To decide upon a suitable range of media to design for, via Google Analytics, I discovered the following viewport sizes to be the most popular:

  1. 320x568
  2. 360x640
  3. 375x667
  4. 414x736
  5. 768x1024
  6. 1366x768

Some dimensions are considered standard, such as an iPad (768x1024), or a typical Android device (360x640).

With the dimensions I create what I refer to as a Container Grid (Fig. 1-4).

The Container Grid's purpose is to enable placement of content. Between breakpoints, any content within the fluid-margin will have a fluid width, whilst content placed across both margins will adhere to the dimensions of a given viewport width. If a screen is wider than all columns, gutters and margins, fluid-margin can be utilised to solve a range of design problems (Fig. 4).
It's important to note that terms such as margin and fluid-margin are not related to CSS, their purpose is purely for design.
