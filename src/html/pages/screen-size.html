To decide upon a suitable range of media to design for, via Google Analytics, I discovered the following viewport sizes to be the most popular:

  1. 320x568
  2. 360x640
  3. 375x667
  4. 414x736
  5. 768x1024
  6. 1366x768

Some dimensions are considered standard, such as an iPad (768x1024), or a typical Android device (360x640).

With the dimensions I create what I refer to as a Container Grid see fig.1-4.

The Container Grid's purpose is to enable placement of content. Between breakpoints, any content within the fluid-margin will have a fluid width, whilst content placed within both margins will adhere to the dimensions of a given viewport width. If a viewport is larger than the width of all columns, gutters and margins, excess space can be utilised to solve a range of design problems. Header, nav or image elements can span the entire viewport width beyond the bounds of the margins fig. 4.

When it comes to re-creating a design using CSS and HTML it removes the need for markup, providing freedom to span across different ranges of columns.
It's important to note these terms are not related to CSS properties. achieving a range of possibilities due to the gutters also being columns. Users of screen sizes not in the list will also receive an excellent experience due to the strategy of including fluid elements [examples needed, e.g. bleeding imagery fluid header and footer]. This gives us the benefits of designing for certainty, whilst creating the impression of fluidity.
