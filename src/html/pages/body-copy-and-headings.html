Body type is the main face used for the body of work. Headings are meant to be distinguished from body copy, either by an increase in size, proximity, style, weight, colour, or other means.

Cleverly arranged headings keep skim-reading a simple exercise; the heading acts as a hook, drawing in the reader.

Select a body size that is large enough from an accessibility point of view, one, which works universally across a range of breakpoints, e.g. 16/24. Check on actual devices for legibility, and test on people. Get feedback and make adjustments where necessary, until you get a combination of font-size, line-height and column-width which feels right.
